Te Tiriti o Waitangi


Signed in 1840, the Treaty of Waitangi was an agreement between Māori chiefs and the British Crown.
The agreement between the parties was to protect the Māori culture and the continuation of Māori’s being able to live in New Zealand. The Treaty also enabled the British Crown to govern New Zealand and represent all interest of all New Zealanders.

What is Tikanga Māori?


Tikanga is a notion of integrating practices and values performed by the natives through generations of knowledge that has been passed down to the next generation. This conception is practised by the natives of Aoteatoa "New Zealand', known as Māori's. Tikanga has multiple meanings once translated into English. These meanings range from cultural aspects through to customs, ethics, formalities and many more.
